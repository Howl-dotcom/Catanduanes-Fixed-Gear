Our Story

We are Catanduanes Fixed Gear — a tight crew of riders born from the winding coastal roads and raw streets of Catanduanes Island. We didn't come from a velodrome. We came from the barangay. We came from the streets.

What started as a handful of riders rolling on single-speeds has grown into a real community movement. Early morning group rides, late-night skid sessions, DIY builds in garages — this is what we do. No sponsors, no gatekeeping. Just gravel, gears, and grit.

We believe in the fixed gear philosophy: simplicity over excess. One gear. Direct drive. No freewheel. Every pedal stroke is intentional. Every skid is earned.

The Philosophy

Fixed gear cycling is more than a style — it's a discipline. You can't coast. You can't disconnect. When the wheel turns, your legs turn. That constant connection between rider and road builds a kind of awareness that no geared bike can replicate.

In the streets of Catanduanes, riding fixed is a statement. It says: I am in control. I choose difficulty over comfort. I choose honesty over flash.

We pass that culture down. New riders, old riders — everyone starts from zero when they clip in for the first time. The street doesn't care about your experience. It only respects your commitment.

Fixie Culture — The Rules
01

No Coasting

A fixie has no freewheel. The cog is fixed directly to the rear hub — if the wheel spins, your legs spin. There is no rest. There is no passive riding. You are always engaged, always connected.

02

The Skid Stop

The iconic fixie brake — lock your legs, resist the pedals, and let the rear tire scream across asphalt. A skid patch is a badge of honor. The longer, the louder, the better. It takes technique, strength, and nerve.

03

Track Stand

Balance at zero miles per hour. No clips, no curb — just raw body control keeping the bike perfectly still. The track stand separates the committed from the curious. Hours of practice for seconds of silence.

04

The Build Culture

Fixie riders build their own bikes. A salvaged frame, mismatched bars, hand-painted rims — every bike tells a story. No two are the same. The build is personal, political, and punk. Your bike is your identity.

05

Street Messenger Roots

Fixed gear culture was born on the streets of New York City with bike messengers who needed reliable, low-maintenance machines. No gears to break, no cables to snap. Just pure mechanical simplicity built for urban survival.

06

Community Over Competition

We ride together. We fix each other's bikes. We share routes, share tools, share knowledge. The CFG crew is not about who's fastest — it's about who shows up. Ride alone, arrive fast. Ride together, arrive far.
